VHDL stands for “very high speed integrated circuits hardware description language”, and ﬁrst developed by the US department of defense in 1980s. It is used to describe the behavior of an electronic circuit. VHDL programs consist of program modules. A module is nothing but a program segment written for some speciﬁc purposes.
